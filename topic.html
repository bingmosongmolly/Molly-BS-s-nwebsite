Why I’m Exploring Learning Analytics

I’m fascinated by learning analytics—the practice of using data to improve how we study and teach. Good analytics doesn’t replace human judgment; it augments it. Simple dashboards can reveal whether a study plan is balanced, where time is being wasted, and which topics deserve extra attention. I’m starting with lightweight tools such as spreadsheets and small JavaScript snippets to track goals, outcomes, and reflection notes. The goal isn’t to collect every metric; it’s to capture a few signals that lead to better decisions week after week.

My approach emphasizes transparency and iteration. I regularly review what I planned versus what actually happened, write a short summary of what worked, and choose one change to test next. Over time, these small adjustments accumulate into real progress. I also care about clear visuals: clean typography, generous spacing, and minimal color accents make information easier to scan. This site is my sandbox for those ideas, and I’ll continue to expand it with examples, notes, and bite-sized experiments anyone can try.

What I’m Tracking

  • Study sessions completed vs. planned each week
  • Topics that caused confusion and how I resolved them
  • One small weekly experiment and its outcome
